Stars unravel following Heiskanen injury in Game 3 of Western 2nd Round

Kraken score 4 goals in 6:12 span after defenseman takes puck to face, leaves in 2nd

by Nicholas J. Cotsonika @cotsonika / NHL.com Columnist

SEATTLE -- The Dallas Stars did not use Miro Heiskanen's injury as an excuse for their 7-2 loss to the Seattle Kraken in Game 3 of the Western Conference Second Round at Climate Pledge Arena on Sunday. But the game changed in an instant when Heiskanen took a puck in the face early in the second period, with Seattle scoring four goals in 6:12 afterward to take a 4-0 lead. And the question now is whether the series changed too. Heiskanen, one of the best defensemen in the NHL, did not return. Dallas trails 2-1 in the best-of-7 series entering Game 4 here on Tuesday (9:30 p.m. ET; ESPN, SN, TVAS).
